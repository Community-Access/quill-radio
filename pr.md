# FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

## Quill Radio 1.0 arrives: the next generation of the ACB Radio Tuner, built for every screen reader, every station on Earth

**A free, open-source Windows app carries forward a beloved community project — and takes it somewhere it could never go before.**

---

For more than a decade, blind and low-vision listeners who wanted ACB Media on their desktop reached for the ACB Radio Tuner — a small, devoted tool built by and for the American Council of the Blind's radio community. It did one thing, and it did that one thing with care. It never chased the wider internet. It never grew a favorites tree, a recorder, or a second life beyond its original ten streams. It didn't have to. It was enough, for a long time, to just play ACB Media reliably and get out of the way.

Today, Community Access is releasing **Quill Radio 1.0** — free, open source, and built from the ground up as the spiritual successor to that project. It plays every ACB Media stream out of the box, with zero setup. And then it keeps going: tens of thousands of stations from every country on the map, a real favorites system with folders as deep as you want them, recording with automatic reconnection when your internet stumbles, a wake-up timer, hardware media key support, and a system tray presence that keeps the music playing while you work. It is, quite simply, everything the original tuner was — plus everything a modern radio app was always missing.

"When I first heard about the ACB Radio Tuner years ago, it felt like someone had finally built something *for* us instead of merely accessible *to* us," said the President of Blind Information Technology Specialists (BITS), an ACB affiliate. "Quill Radio takes that same spirit and asks a bigger question: what if the whole world's radio worked that way? What if recording a show, organizing your favorite stations, and waking up to your favorite program were all just as effortless as pressing Enter on a station name? This isn't a replacement for what we loved. It's what we always hoped it would grow into."

### From ten streams to the whole dial

Quill Radio opens with your favorite stations tree already focused — arrow down, press Enter, and you're listening. No dialogs to dismiss, no mouse required, nothing standing between you and the sound. The bundled **ACB Media** directory carries all ten of ACB's own streams — Mainstream, The Trove, Cafe, Community, Live, Special, and the rest — playable the moment you install, exactly as the original tuner promised.

But where the ACB Radio Tuner stopped, Quill Radio was just getting started. **Browse Stations** opens the live, community-maintained RadioBrowser directory: tens of thousands of stations from every country, searchable by name, genre, language, or tag, with a Test button so you can preview before you commit. Found a stream on a website that isn't in any directory? **Find Streams from a Website** scans the page and offers up whatever it finds. Paste a URL directly if you already know exactly what you want. However a station reaches you, it becomes a first-class favorite with one keystroke.

### A favorites system that grows the way your listening does

The original tuner had a flat list. Quill Radio has folders — as many, and as deep, as you want. Build "News," then "News > Morning" and "News > Evening" inside it, with a Ctrl+Shift+E away from creating any of them wherever you need them, before a single station lives there. Rename any station to what *you* call it — your custom name follows it everywhere, from the main window to the tray to the Recently Played list. Delete a folder and its stations don't vanish with it; they step safely back to the top level. Nothing you build is ever one keystroke from destroyed.

Every favorite remembers its own volume. Stations are mastered wildly differently — one whispers, the next one shouts — and Quill Radio only makes you fix that once per station, forever.

### Recording that survives the real internet

Recording in Quill Radio isn't a fragile afterthought. Start a capture and, if your connection drops mid-show, ffmpeg first tries to ride out the gap; if the drop is real, Quill Radio waits, reconnects, and resumes into a cleanly numbered part file — with the number of attempts and the spacing between them entirely yours to tune. Record what's already playing, or record a second station while you listen to something else entirely — the recorder never needed the player to begin with. Schedule a show once, daily, or weekly, picking straight from your favorites list instead of retyping a URL from memory. And the **Recordings** window shows the whole life cycle at a glance: recording now with its size growing live, recorded and ready, or scheduled and waiting — with Play, Stop, Open in Folder, and Remove all one keystroke away.

### An appliance, when you want it to be one

Check **Resume Last Station on Launch** once, and Quill Radio becomes exactly what a radio should be: something you turn on. **Play Last Station** (Ctrl+L) picks up wherever you left off without any navigation at all. The **Wake-Up Timer** is the sleep timer's twin — pick a favorite, pick a time, once or every day, and the station starts itself. Hardware media keys control playback system-wide, even when the app is tucked away in the tray, with its own icon and a live now-playing tooltip.

And no matter where a stream comes from — a favorite, ACB Media, Recently Played, or a fresh search — starting it always silences whatever else was playing, radio or podcast, everywhere in the app. You never hear two things fighting for your attention.

### Built to be heard, not just used

Every single thing Quill Radio does, it says out loud, through the same announcement engine that powers QUILL itself — clean, accurate, and compatible with JAWS, NVDA, and Windows Narrator without ever stealing focus. The **Command Palette** (Ctrl+Shift+P) puts every Quill Radio command behind one searchable list. **What's Playing** (Ctrl+T) speaks the current track or show title straight from the stream's own metadata, the moment you ask.

Quill Radio shares one data store with QUILL and QUILL Cast: favorites, history, recordings, schedules, and settings all travel together, everywhere. Favorite a station here, and it's already a favorite in QUILL. Set a wake-up timer in QUILL, and it fires here. Nothing is ever set up twice.

### Two flavors, everything included

Quill Radio ships as a full system installer or a truly portable zip — extract it to a USB stick and the whole radio, favorites and all, travels with you. Both bundle ffmpeg for recording; nothing downloads at install time or runtime, ever. **Help > Get FFmpeg** stands ready as a safety net on the rare chance it's ever needed. In-app **Check for Updates** knows exactly which flavor you're running and fetches the matching artifact automatically, with spoken progress the whole way.

### We want to hear from you

Quill Radio is, and always will be, free. It is also young, and it grows from the people who use it. **Help > Report a Bug** files an issue directly from inside the app — no GitHub account required — stamped with Quill Radio's own version number so we always know exactly what you were running. If you'd rather write to us directly, or you have an idea for where this should go next, open an issue at the project's GitHub repository, or reach out to Community Access. Every report shapes what ships next; that has been true since the first line of QUILL was written, and it's just as true here.

### A note on where this came from

Quill Radio is offered with real gratitude to the American Council of the Blind and to everyone who built and maintained the original ACB Radio Tuner over the years. That project proved something important: that a radio app designed specifically for blind listeners, by people who understood exactly what that meant, could be simple, dependable, and genuinely loved. Quill Radio doesn't replace that legacy. It's an attempt to honor it — by taking the same care and pointing it at the whole dial.

**About Quill Radio**

Quill Radio is a free, open-source, screen-reader-first internet radio application for Windows, built by Community Access and sharing its codebase with the QUILL writing environment. It is not code-signed; Windows SmartScreen may show a warning on first run — choose "More info," then "Run anyway."
